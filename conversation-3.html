KEY THEMES

What's shaping the Innovation Lab

01

The Innovation Lab Concept

Rob proposes launching a "GoDoGood AI Innovation Lab in DC" in partnership with TJ/Workhorse. Not a new venture — a collaboration. Weekly calls, AI efficiency updates, implementation into agency processes. A public-facing hub for AI + design thinking.

02

Personal Brand as Anchor

Better to position the lab around TJ's personal brand (tjcichecki.com) than Workhorse initially. Articles already live, credibility established. Rob's clients can point to TJ as the Lab lead. The studio aesthetic — analog workshop producing cutting-edge tech — is the selling point.

03

Two Target Verticals

Nonprofits: purpose-driven orgs needing $10–20K brand + website packages. Fast start, simpler needs, huge volume. EdTech: 30–40% of GoDoGood's work. VC/PE-backed platforms with product teams, AI-obsessed, $100K+ budgets for retainers and rebrands.

04

Productized Packages

Nonprofit Starter: brand + site + docs for $10–20K. WordPress-based (new Claude Code integration). Two conversations (sales + kickoff brain dump), then largely automated. AI Consulting Audit: $10K engagement to assess marketing ops and deliver board-ready recommendations.

05

The Monetization Question

Key open question: how does TJ get compensated beyond project fees? If Rob sells a $10K package, what's the split? Pricing strategy and go-to-market model still being worked out between the partners.

06

The Site-Copying Skill

TJ built a Claude Code skill that fully replicates a website — pulled GoDoGood's site, rebuilt it locally, applied recommended design changes. Potential to streamline the productized workflow: less boilerplate, faster iteration on existing sites.

THE WORDPRESS PLAY

Why WordPress now

WordPress + Claude Code Integration

New WordPress Studio allows local development with Claude Code, then deployment to WordPress hosting. Same sales points GoDoGood has been using for years — no need to pitch a new tech stack.

Brand Built Into the Backend

Brand guidelines, style documentation, and content strategy live on the site itself. Clients get their own brand reference without a separate PDF or Figma file.

Two Conversations, Then Automation

The human work is the sales call and the kickoff brain dump. Everything after that — site build, content population, brand documentation — can be largely automated from that captured context.

WHAT'S DIFFERENT

Versus Accenture & McKinsey

Market

Nonprofit-Specific Positioning

Accenture and McKinsey serve Fortune 500. This targets the 20,000 nonprofits and purpose-driven orgs that can't afford a $1M consulting engagement but desperately need AI guidance.

Approach

Design-Led, Not Systems-Led

Big consultancies focus on enterprise systems integration. This starts with brand, design, and marketing workflows — the creative side that nonprofits and EdTech companies actually care about.

Price

Accessible Price Points

$10K for a structured AI audit. $10–20K for a brand + website package. Not $500K annual retainers. Sized for the organizations that need it most.

Proof

Built, Not Theorized

Everything being sold has already been built and tested. The process documentation site, the click-to-edit admin, the site-copying skill — these aren't proposals, they're working tools.
