KEY THEMES

What emerged from Day 2

01

The CMS Is the Conversation

TJ demoed the click-to-edit admin panel — hover over any element, click it, tell Claude what to change, and it updates live. No Sanity, no WordPress admin. The editing interface is a chat window.

02

Minimum Viable Brand

Not every nonprofit needs a $30K brand overhaul. The concept of an MVB — minimum viable brand — gives clients a logo, a site, and enough visual identity to be credible. Build on it later.

03

The $5K–$10K Sweet Spot

Small nonprofits with limited budgets are the entry point. One conversation, two to three days of build, deployed and done. The agentic model makes these profitable instead of loss leaders.

04

Gateway to Retainers

Websites are the entry point, but the real value is ongoing: donor campaigns, social creative, annual reports, email newsletters. Get in the door with a site, stay for the mission.

05

Scale Through People, Not Code

TJ innovates and builds the tools. Then onboards designers who execute using those tools. The creative director role shifts from production to system design and quality assurance.

06

Micro-Tools for Clients

Beyond websites: branded social graphic generators, content calendar tools, asset management. Each client gets bespoke micro-tools built into their site that solve their specific operational problems.

LIVE DEMO

The admin panel in action

During the call, TJ demonstrated the click-to-edit interface he built in roughly an hour. The system works: hover any content block to see its label, click to select it, describe your change in natural language, and Claude updates the site. No CMS login. No content models. No deploy pipeline. Just conversation.

Annotation Overlay

Every content block has a hover tooltip showing its path. Click any element to reference it in the chat.

Conversational Editing

Type what you want changed in plain English. Claude reads the current content, makes the edit, and shows you old vs. new.

One-Click Publish

When you're happy with changes, hit Publish. The site rebuilds from templates and deploys to production in seconds.

No External CMS

Content lives in JSON files. The editing interface is a Netlify Function calling Claude. No Sanity subscription, no WordPress plugins, no vendor lock-in.

THE CLIENT PIPELINE

How a project flows

01

Lead Comes In

GoDoGood's biz dev team qualifies the lead. Budget, timeline, scope. Is this a $5K site refresh or a $30K capital campaign?

02

Rob Has the Honest Conversation

Are they ignorable? Does their brand resonate? Is the real problem messaging, not design? This filters out projects that aren't ready.

03

Brief Handoff

The qualifying call is recorded, transcribed, and processed into a brief. TJ gets the context without a separate debrief meeting.

04

TJ Builds End-to-End

One conversation with the client. Capture requirements, brand direction, content. Then build: brand, site, and documentation. Days, not months.

05

Client Edits via Chat

Revisions happen through the admin panel. Client says what they want changed. No back-and-forth email chains. No design review rounds.

06

Upsell to Ongoing

The site is the gateway. Donor campaigns, annual reports, social strategy, email newsletters — the relationship expands from there.

THE ELEPHANT IN THE ROOM

What about WordPress?

WordPress isn't going away

Some clients will always want WordPress. Their IT team requires it. Their webmaster knows it. That's fine — AI tools can accelerate WordPress builds too.

WordPress is adding AI features

GoDoGood's paid media lead in South Africa confirmed they're experimenting with WordPress AI tools. The CMS landscape is shifting everywhere.

The agentic model is CMS-agnostic

The real innovation isn't the output format. It's the process: one person, end-to-end, conversation-driven. Whether the output is static HTML, WordPress, or something else is secondary.

Instead of a PDF brand guide, the guidelines live on the site itself. Colors, typography, usage rules — documented, versioned, always current.

Tool

Content Calendar Automation
